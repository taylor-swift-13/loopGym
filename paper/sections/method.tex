\section{Method}\label{sec:method}
%% ============================================================

\paragraph{Notation.}
For a program $P = (\phi_{\mathrm{pre}}, B, S)$, a \emph{rollout} $A$ is one candidate clause set produced by one policy sample, and a \emph{group} $\mathcal{A} = \{A_1, \dots, A_G\}$ is the set of rollouts sampled for $P$ in one RL step.
The sampler emits positives $\mathcal{P}$ (reachable loop-head states, each carrying its run's input valuation) and negatives $\mathcal{N}$ grouped into \emph{trace units}: each unit is one impossible history under a concrete input, witnessed by one or more states.
$\mathrm{surv}(X)$ denotes the subset of clause set $X$ that survives the filter cascade of \S\ref{sec:method:reward}, and $\mathrm{rej}(X) \subseteq \mathcal{N}$ the trace units rejected by $X$ --- a unit is rejected iff some surviving clause is false at one of its witness states.

%% ------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Sampler: Positives and Truthful Negatives}
\label{sec:method:sampler}

The sampler is deliberately blind to the postcondition: it parses only the precondition, guard, and body.
It instruments the loop to print the loop-head valuation on entry, at every iteration, and at exit, compiles with \texttt{gcc}, and executes over a deterministic, precondition-checked input sweep (edge and moderate magnitudes, systematic tier combinations, plus a few large-magnitude probes that widen the input envelope).
Loops run to their real exit; runs that hit the iteration cap are flagged and excluded from any range conclusion.
The observed loop-head states are the positives.

Negatives are \emph{truthful by construction}: each family carries its own unreachability argument, and is emitted only where that argument holds.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=2pt, topsep=2pt]
\item \textbf{Relational perturbations} apply $\pm\{1,2\}$ single-axis and pairwise steps to trace states whose next few successors were all sampled; since every reachable in-window neighbor is known, a surviving perturbation is genuinely off the reachable manifold.  They expose missing laws such as $x + y = n$.
\item \textbf{Over-runs} execute the real body past a genuine exit: they preserve every (even non-linear) relational law while leaving the reachable range, and each continuation forms one trace unit.
\item \textbf{Escapes} move one variable outside its observed range by ladder steps, kept only when they escape; since traces run to the real exit, escapees are unreachable.  Together with over-runs they expose missing range facts.
\end{itemize}
Three vetoes guarantee label correctness --- a mislabeled (actually reachable) negative would penalize exactly the true invariants.
States observed in any run are never negatives; states that could constitute a fresh loop entry under their input (parameters at pre-values, initialized locals at their initializers, precondition satisfied) are dropped; and variables tainted by nondeterminism (\texttt{unknown()} values, control dependence on nondeterministic branches, and their data-flow closure) are never perturbed.
A nondeterministic guard disables the over-run and escape families entirely --- the loop can always run longer, so no range is sound.
The sampler thus degrades soundly: where no argument applies it emits few or no negatives rather than poisoned ones.

\subsection{Reward: Two Scores over Prover-Backed Survivors}
\label{sec:method:reward}

Each rollout is first reduced to its sound inductive core by a three-stage cascade.
A \emph{syntax scrub} runs the Frama-C kernel on the annotated program and drops the exact \texttt{loop invariant} lines it rejects, so one malformed clause cannot void a whole candidate set.
A \emph{positive pre-filter} then drops clauses that reference out-of-scope identifiers or are falsified by some sampled positive --- a cheap, prover-free approximation that only removes clauses the prover would reject anyway, saving Houdini iterations.
Finally \emph{Houdini pruning}~\citep{flanagan2001houdini} over Frama-C/WP~\citep{framac} retains the greatest inductive subset.
Because survival ends in a real prover, soundness never rests on the sampled states: predicates overfitted to the sample fail initiation or consecution for unconstrained inputs and are removed by proof.

Surviving clauses are scored against the negative trace units:
\begin{align}
\mathrm{base}(A) = \frac{|\mathrm{rej}(\mathrm{surv}(A))|}{|\mathcal{N}|},
\qquad
\mathrm{marg}(A) = \frac{\bigl|\mathrm{rej}(\mathrm{surv}(U)) \setminus \mathrm{rej}(\mathrm{surv}(U \setminus A))\bigr|}{|\mathcal{N}|},
\end{align}
where $U = \bigcup_{A \in \mathcal{A}} A$ is the group union, and the reward is the weighted sum $r(A) = w_b\,\mathrm{base}(A) + w_m\,\mathrm{marg}(A)$ (we use $w_b = w_m = 0.5$).
Base measures how much of the impossible space the rollout alone excludes; the marginal term is its ablation gain over the rest of the group --- group-relative credit in the spirit of GRPO~\citep{shao2024deepseekmath}.
The marginal term also protects test-time diversity: repeated sampling is known to place correct invariants in the candidate pool~\citep{chakraborty2023ranking,wu2024lam4inv}, while RL fine-tuning tends to sharpen the sampling distribution~\citep{yue2025does}; since $\mathrm{marg}$ pays only for what the rest of the group does not already exclude, rollouts restating one another earn nothing, and the group optimum is a set of complementary summaries.
Rejection tests are pure Python with C integer semantics (matching the compiled traces), so the only heavyweight cost is the prover; the distinct clause sets a group needs (per-rollout, union, ablations) are memoized and verified in parallel, and each WP run discharges its goals concurrently.
The group additionally receives $\mathrm{batch} = |\mathrm{rej}(\mathrm{surv}(U))| / |\mathcal{N}|$ and a re-roll flag when it falls below a threshold.

\subsection{Inference: Closed-Book Generation with Certified Repair}
\label{sec:method:inference}

At inference time the policy is prompted with the program after postcondition stripping and asked for the strongest inductive ACSL loop invariants.
The framework unions the clauses of $n$ rollouts, deduplicates, and applies the same cascade as the reward --- syntax scrub, pre-filter, Houdini --- ending in Frama-C/WP verification of the re-annotated program.
Repair is Houdini-style subtraction rather than conversational self-correction: unsound clauses are dropped by the pruner, not re-prompted; on failure the framework re-rolls a bounded number of times.
Training and inference share one canonical sampler configuration and one filter cascade, so the reward optimized during RL is exactly the quantity the deployed pipeline measures.
